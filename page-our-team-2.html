Daniel Engels

Founder / General Manager

My name is Daniel Engels, born and raised in Redwood City, California. I majored in Political Science at the University of California Riverside. Additionally, I have been working in my family’s business for over a decade. The reason I founded Create Opportunity is really an ode to my background. In the early 1980s, my grandfather, Robert Gray, started a business known as Gray’s Paints. Growing up and working there from the age of 15 to my mid twenties, watching how hard my grandfather and uncle worked, left an invaluable impression on me. But, I always asked myself, “How did he start the business?”

As it turns out, our family dentist gave my grandfather a personal loan of $5,000 and pooled a few of his friends to support my grandfather’s business dream. This relatively small personal loan transformed the lives of an entire family. The paint store has since grown from a 600 square foot store to four locations serving the greater peninsula just south of San Francisco. Inspired by my grandfather’s story, I founded Create Opportunity to support local entrepreneurs and small businesses with zero interest microloans to help them grow and serve their communities.

Stephen Carico

Marketing Director / Visual Designer

I’ve always had a passion for technology. As a kid I would draft up plans for iPods that could play both music and videos, draw out sample cards for the next big trading card game, and design blueprints to build the first hovercraft, which Elon Musk and I would now mutually agree is unfeasible. However, it wasn't until years later that I took my first Entrepreneurship class and realized that interest went beyond technology - at its core my passion was for creating.

I then enrolled in a Business Plan competition with an idea that in essence was a buzz marketing service that I called Webscope. Even though I didn’t win the competition and left as a semifinalist, my passion for creation did not stop there. I continued my research into business while attending UCR, starting a Fraternity, conceptualizing and planning multiple fundraising events for the V Foundation for Cancer Research, and recruiting team members to work on tech ventures I found exciting at the time. It was here that I realized that most of these ventures failed for two reasons: I wasn't properly leading my team to success and I had no experience with coding. My mentality therefore became this: in order to reach my goals, I first needed to learn how to properly run a business and manage a team.

After graduation I joined a nationally recognized Management Trainee program, where I got promoted to Assistant Manager within a year of employment. When I felt confident in my leadership, communication skills, and business experience, I decided that it was time to navigate back to my original passion: technology.

So, I joined the Actualize programming boot camp and apprenticeship to learn the hard skills needed for such an industry. I learned not only the proper languages and frameworks for full-stack development, but also what it took to think as a legitimate programmer. Now, I’m seeking a team of like-minded individuals to continue my journey of creating and developing something fun, innovative, and exciting.
